Authentication and authorization serve as the core security pillars of the Red Hat build of Keycloak. They are designed to effectively manage user identity and control access within your applications.

To understand these pillars, let’s look at their specific roles.

First, authentication is responsible for verifying a user's identity. This is handled through highly configurable authentication flows, which allow you to define flexible login requirements and implement custom session management to suit your environment.

Second, authorization is used to enforce access control. It utilizes a combination of Role-Based Access Control, or RBAC, and permission-based models. This ensures that users are restricted to accessing only the specific resources they are authorized to use.

Finally, a key strength of the system is customization. You have the capability to tailor authentication sessions to meet your organization's specific security policies and the unique needs of your applications.
